Human Rights Award Committee Inaugurated

Group Photo of the Members of The Human Rights Award Committee

The AACC Human Rights Award Committee has been inaugurated today in Nairobi, Kenya by our General Secretary, Rev Dr. Fidon Mwombeki. AACC is instituting a biennial Pan African award to recognise the contributions of Individuals and Organisations across the continent whose focus and life mission is to champion Human Rights, Good Governance, and the Rule of Law.

Human rights are the basic rights and freedoms that belong to every person in the world, from birth until death. These rights apply regardless of your origin, belief, or way of life. These basic rights are based on shared values like dignity, fairness, equality, respect, and independence. The issue of Human Rights and the Rule of Law has been placed on the African agenda in recent years as part of the overall democracy drive.

In view of this, the AACC through the “Enhancing capacities of the AACC to heighten its advocacy role for Human Dignity & Justice in the continent” Project is instituting a biennial Pan African award to recognise the contributions of Individuals and Organisations across the continent whose focus and life mission is to champion Human Rights, Good Governance and the Rule of Law.

This is to encourage African Citizens to commit to the transformation of the Continent in attaining inclusive and sustainable economic growth and development.

To achieve this objective, the AACC through its Advocacy Advisory Group has created an Independent Awards Committee. This has the responsibility of ensuring the implementation of a credible and transparent nomination, screening, and selection process and also identifying winners who will receive the award at our 12th General Assembly and 60th Anniversary Celebrations in Abuja, Nigeria.

All Africa Conference of Churches (AACC)

AACC is a continental ecumenical body that accounts for over 140 million Christians across the continent.
