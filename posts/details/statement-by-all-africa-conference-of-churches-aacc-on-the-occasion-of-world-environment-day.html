Nairobi: 5 June 2022

STATEMENT BY ALL AFRICA CONFERENCE OF CHURCHES (AACC)

ON THE OCCASION OF WORLD ENVIRONMENT DAY

“The Welfare of the Earth is our Welfare (Jeremiah: 29:7)”

Today, the 5th June 2022, the AACC joins the global community to commemorate World Environment Day under the theme #OnlyOneEarth. As people of faith, we take this moment to deeply reflect on the gift of the environment (ecosystem) as scriptures teach in Genesis 1:26-28. We challenge ourselves, the communities, the leaders of Africa, and the world as a whole, to do better in our utilization of the environment to guarantee the future of the generation to come. This important day comes against a backdrop of accelerating climate impacts among other environmental challenges.

While the AACC takes note of many environmental challenges such as desertification; deforestation and land degradation; air and water pollution as well as loss of biodiversity. AACC made a strategic decision in 2019 to contribute towards “Addressing the Impact of Climate Change” in line with the Sustainable Development Goal 13. Therefore, the Church leaders have been participating in critical national, regional, and global processes such as the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change’s Conference of Parties.

As we commemorate World Environment Day, we are alive to the fact that African countries are among the most affected by Climate Change. The rise in the average global temperature is predicted to occur sooner and faster and Africa will be affected more than other parts of the world as the continent’s economies rely on climate-dependent sectors such as agriculture and its capacities to cope and adapt are generally limited.

 Environmental disasters such as droughts, floods, reduced crop yields, and livestock capacities, as well as the spreading of climate-sensitive diseases such as malaria and cholera, continue to threaten the African continent. The Climate Change crisis has further thrown Africa into unending conflicts among farming and herding communities.

The COVID 19 Pandemic and the ugly events currently happening in Russia and Ukraine and their effects on many African economies have exposed our vulnerability as African people because of our poor adaptive capacities.

As an ecumenical body representing over 140 million Christians in Africa, the AACC reiterates its commitment to a safe, secure and resilient environment. In the spirit of the Paris Agreement, we continue to encourage our members to work closely with their national Governments for the fulfillment of Nationally Determined Contributions to the reduction of greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions to achieve and keep global temperatures below the 1.5-degree Celsius target. The Church also remains key in the implementation of adaptation and mitigation measures at the local level.

We further commit to work with our African Governments as the continent prepares to host COP27 in Sharm el-Sheikh, Egypt. We call for all nations that are signatories to the Paris Agreement to focus on the implementation of the various initiatives on adaptation, mitigation, and climate financing. Thenations should show how they will, through legislation, policies, and programs, and throughout all jurisdictions and sectors, begin putting the Paris Agreement into practice in their home countries.

On our part, as we commemorate this year’s Environment Day, we look forward to our High-Level Faith Leaders Continental Consultative meeting to be held from 20 to 24 June 2022 in Nairobi. African Faith leaders will converge to reflect, discuss and reimagine a future where forces of environmental destruction are confronted for a better future for all. An African Faith Actors Network for Climate Justice will be established to coordinate our work on climate change.
