Suspend All Other Spending And Fight Drought Crisis, Horn of Africa Governments Told


Governments of countries in the Horn of Africa have been urged to suspend all other development priorities and direct budget and human resources to respond to the humanitarian crisis caused by the drought that has engulfed the region.
In a statement released in Nairobi by twenty-two major humanitarian organizations, multilateral and international private financial institutions were urged to suspend all debt repayments due to them in 2022 and 2023, to allow those funds to be invested in responding to this humanitarian crisis.
“The humanitarian crisis must be responded to at the urgently needed speed and scale,” they stated, and called for available funding for the crisis to be released to the frontline immediately.
The organisations gathered in Nairobi to express their “profound concern regarding the forgotten humanitarian crisis engulfing the Horn of Africa”. They decried the fact that in a world where there was enough food to feed everyone, people were still dying of hunger and millions were threatened by famine.
The organisations comprised All Africa Conference of Churches (AACC), ActionAid Int. Kenya, Action Against Hunger, ADRA Kenya, African Youth Commission, AMREF, ASAL humanitarian Network, CAFOD, Catholic Relief Services, Christian Aid, Cordaid, Hellen Keller Foundation, HelpAge, Pan African Climate Justice Alliance, and Plan International.
Others were International Aid Services Kenya, International Alert, Medecins Sans Frontieres, MISEREOR, SOS Children’s Home, Voluntary Services Overseas and World Resources Institute.
In their statement, the organisations stated that at least 36 million people were currently affected by the drought, a figure that had almost doubled since July 2022. They noted that about 4.6 million children and a million pregnant and lactating women in the drought areas were acutely malnourished.
The statement said that over 8.9 million livestock have perished, forcing communities to migrate and encroach in areas where other communities live.
“Rivers and water pans where farmers were drawing water and livestock owners were watering their animals have all dried up,” the statement said.
The organisations decried the fact that scientists have repeatedly warned of insufficient rainfall and prolonged drought attributed to the changing climate.
“We are saddened to see the results of this inaction despite numerous warnings, and yet global leaders continue to drag their feet when it comes to honoring climate financing pledges,” they stated. They added that the drought crisis was a manifestation of the failure to build resilient nations in the face of a climate crisis.
They made an urgent call to the leadership of governments and regional bodies who were implementing national and regional climate change policies and the recently approved African Union Climate Change Response Strategy and Action Plan 2022-2032 to fast-track their actions.
They also called on the forthcoming COP27 in Egypt in November 2022 to move from rhetoric to demonstrable action by ensuring the target of the 50:50 split between mitigation and adaptation finance was not only met, but surpassed in favor of adaptation.
