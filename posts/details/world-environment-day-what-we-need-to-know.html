World Environment Day: What We Need to Know

“The Welfare of the Earth is our Welfare (Jeremiah: 29:7)”

  1. Introduction

World Environment Day (WED) is celebrated on June 5 every year across the globe. The United Nations Environment Program (UNEP) organizes events for the WED every year to spread awareness and encourage action for the protection of the environment. People of Faith such as members of the All Africa Conference of Churches (AACC) also join the world in celebrating this important day. There are a few historical and contemporary facts that we need to know about this day and they are highlighted below.

  1. History and Significance

In 1972, the first United Nations Conference on Environment was held. At the conference, a series of principles, the Stockholm Declaration and Action plan and several other resolutions were adopted by participants. That Conference also marked the creation of the United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP) and the idea of WED was also born. It was born out of the understanding that human beings need to stand up to protect the air, land and water, on which they all depend.

Since 1974, the WED has been celebrated annually in the whole world through the official leadership of UNEP. The day has become an important platform for promoting progress on the environmental dimensions of the Sustainable Development Goals and other key global guidelines on environment issues.

  1. Why should we celebrate the WED?

The WED is a significant event for recognizing environmental efforts by different stakeholders and celebrating the importance of protecting the environment for human existence. Clearly, the environment is currently under threat from human activities and it needs to be protected by all of us. The environmental issues confronting the world today require immediate attention and effective action by all stakeholders. The WED gives us an opportunity to reflect on improving our relationship with the environment and protecting the planet we all live in.

  1. Why the Church needs to Celebrate the WED

Environmental issues have continued to be critical matters of discussion in the Church and public space. The Church’s particular interest is informed by the fact that environmental issues raise ethical and moral questions that beg for responses that will offer guidance to its members and shape public agenda. The Church is driven by the consciousness of its role in care for the creation (Genesis 1:26,28). Caring brings the Church into communion with the Creator (Job 12:7,8). The Church is further challenged to care for creation from economic, health and social justice perspectives, hence the need to respond to environmental challenges facing humanity. Therefore, the WED presents a strategic moment for the Church to deeply reflect about its role in raising awareness on environmental issues of concern.

  1. The WED 2022 Theme

The first WED was observed with the slogan “Only One Earth” 50 years ago. It became strategic for WED 2022 global campaign to use the theme #OnlyOneEarth. The theme is meant for stakeholders to call for transformative changes to policies and choices to enable cleaner, greener, and sustainable lives in harmony with nature.

The WED is hosted by a different country every year where official celebrations and meetings take place. The host for 2022 celebrations is Sweden. The choice of the venue was also historically significant hence the 2022 meeting is named “Stockholm+50: a healthy planet for the prosperity of all – our responsibility, our opportunity”. It runs from 2 to 3 June 2022.

  1. A Call to Celebration

The AACC encourages its membership to join the world in celebrating this important day. While the ACCC takes note of many environment challenges such as desertification; deforestation and land degradation; air and water pollution and loss of biodiversity, its current strategic focus is on “Addressing the impact of Climate Change” in line with the Sustainable Development Goal 13. This explains why the AACC will officially commission the Africa Faith Actors Network for Climate Justice on 24th June 2022 in Nairobi to spearhead our work on Climate Change. The AACC is already running a social media campaign under the theme “The Welfare of the Earth is Our Welfare” within the context of the WED 2022 celebrations. Members are further encouraged to participate in the campaign through liking and retweeting the key messages being shared.

By Dr Tinashe Gumbo

(AACC Executive Secretary, Economic and Ecological Justice)
